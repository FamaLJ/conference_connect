The Blueprint

30th Annual Black Management Association Conference

Saturday October 29th - 10:00am -5:00pm

James L. Allen Center Evanston, IL

Each year, Kellogg’s Black Management Association Conference brings together students, alumni, faculty and executives to discuss the importance of diversity in thought, experiences and leadership through the lens of the business world. Previous conference speakers include: then U.S. Senator Barack Obama, former CMO of Kraft Deanie Elsner, CNN reporter Soledad O’Brien, and former CEO of the Dallas Mavericks Terdema Ussery.

In celebration of the 30th anniversary, our goal for this year is to pay tribute to the history of the conference, while leveraging the experiences of our speakers to envision a blueprint for success in the future.

Keynote Speakers

Channing Dungey

CEO, ABC

Dungey is an American television executive who in 2016 became the first African American president of ABC Entertainment Group. This also makes her the first black president of a major broadcast TV network.

She began her career in entertainment as a development assistant for Davis Entertainment at 20th Century Fox. She later joined Warner Bros. as a production executive, where she helped develop and supervise a number of commercially successful films including Bridges of Madison County, Heat, The Matrix, and Devil’s Advocate.[

Stewart Butterfield

CEO, Slack

Before building his $2.8 billion startup Slack, which many consider the fastest-growing business app ever, Stewart Butterfield ran another hot startup called Flickr, which was sold to Yahoo for over $20 million.

Butterfield is an avid supporter of the power of diverse teams and co-authored his company’s inaugural diversity report in September, 2015 and touches on the idea that hiring diverse candidates isn’t enough; rather, changing the culture must be part of the goal.

About the

Black Management Association

The Black Management Association (BMA) provides academic and professional career support for its members, while stimulating personal growth in Kellogg’s student body. Our organization leads a number of events to promote dialogue about the value of diversity in business and to provide diverse perspectives on society, enriching the broader Kellogg community.

The BMA provides support and developmental opportunities for Kellogg students over the course of each school year. This ranges from company sponsored events that introduce students to career opportunities, to social events that help establish a sense of community and belonging. Our organization also joins forces with other groups on campus to help shape leaders that value diversity and will help to create more inclusive workplaces over the course of their careers.
